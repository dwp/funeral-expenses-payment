Get help with funeral costs (Funeral Expenses Payment)

What you'll get

Your Funeral Expenses Payment will not usually cover all of the costs of a funeral. The average Funeral Expenses Payment is around £1,800 compared to the average cost of a Funeral in England and Wales of £3,900 

Funeral Expenses Payment can help pay for some of the costs of the following:

  • burial fees for a particular plot
  • cremation fees, including the cost of the doctor’s certificate
  • travel to arrange or go to the funeral
  • the cost of moving the body within the UK, if it’s being moved more than 50 miles
  • death certificates or other documents

Costs you'll get help with

Burial or cremation cost

The payment will usually cover the cost of burial or cremation in the local area where the person who died lived. If this cost is paid for by a funeral plan, you will not get this payment.

We can only work out exactly how much you'll get when we know where the person lived. This is because different locations have different burial and cremation costs. You can find the burial or cermation costs of a local council.

Funeral costs

You can get up to £1,000 for any other funeral expenses, such as funeral director’s fees, flowers or the coffin. If you’re using a funeral director, you can ask them how much these expenses will be.

If the person who died had a pre-paid funeral plan, you can only get up to £120 to help pay for items not covered by their plan.

Your travel costs

You can get help towards travel costs for one return journey to either the:

  • place where you're arranging the funeral
  • funeral location

This includes travel by bus, train and car. You can sometimes get help towards travel by taxi, boat and plane. It does not include travel in a funeral car.

Other costs you may get help with

Transport costs to move the person who died

If you need to move the person who died more than 50 miles, you can get help towards the cost of this.

You'll have to pay for the first 50 miles of any journey. We can only help pay for the travel over 50 miles, as long as the travel is within the UK. Make sure the funeral director puts the full travel distance onto your bill. This includes the distance of any return journeys.

To work out how much you’ll get we will contact your funeral director or use your funeral bill.

Document costs

You can also get help with paying for:

  • death certificates to release money belonging to the person who died so that it can be used towards the cost of the funeral
  • medical certificates needed to bury or cremate someone

Medical costs

You can get help with the cost of certain medical procedures needed before the funeral can go ahead. For example, if a pacemaker needs to be removed before the person who died can be cremated.

What else affects how much you can get

If there is money available from the person who died, it may reduce your Funeral Expenses Payment.

This includes:

  • money the person who died had in bank accounts
  • funeral plans
  • lump-sum payments from pensions or life insurance

Examples of how much you could get

There are examples of how much you could get based on different funeral costs.

How you'll be paid your Funeral Expenses Payment

Funeral Expenses Payment is paid into your bank, building society or credit union account if you’ve already paid for the funeral.

The money will be paid directly to the organiser of the funeral (for example, the funeral director) if you have not paid yet.

If money becomes available from the estate of the person who died

If money becomes available from the estate of the person who died, we will deduct your Funeral Expenses Payment from it. We will recover this money from the estate directly. We will not ask you to pay it back.

The estate includes any money or property they had but not a house or personal things left to a widow, widower or surviving civil partner.
